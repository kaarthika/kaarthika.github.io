white/off-white

Beril, Stacy, Shree, Anna (all Architecture '20), Alec (Sociology '20), and I created white/off-white for the 2020 Anderson School of Architecture Archi-Arts Charette. The theme of the Charette was duo. white/off-white breaks up the room into two spaces white/off-white was a collaborative effort between me, a sociology student, and 4 architecture students for the 2020 Rice Architecture Archi- Arts Charette. We were tasked with creating an interactive site-specific installation that responded to the event's theme, Duo. Our work was inspired by a definition of duo as *two complete wholes working in harmony*. We explored this interpretation by dividing the installation into two unique spaces with a suspended white & off-white checkered sheet. This created two dual viewing experiences (outside, above, below, inside). The two colors were chosen because the subtlety of duality â€” the two colors are indistinguishable until brought together.
